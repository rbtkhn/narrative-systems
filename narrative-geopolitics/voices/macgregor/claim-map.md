## Claim Notes

### Elite Overreach and Widening War

Macgregor is most valuable when the question is whether leadership decisions are opening new theaters, widening commitments, or escalating without a credible off-ramp.

Open review:

- Which warnings recur across the whole slice?
- Which escalation claims later converge with archive-confirmed events?

### Regime Fragility and Leadership Pressure

Macgregor often treats strategic failure as politically destabilizing for leadership. This can be analytically useful, but it needs careful separation between pressure indicators and outcome certainty.

Open review:

- Which claims are about rising pressure versus imminent removal?
- What evidence threshold should be met before using these claims in synthesis?

### Narrative-Reality Gap

This family covers Macgregor's recurring insistence that official or media narratives have detached from battlefield, geopolitical, or institutional limits.

Open review:

- Which gaps are supported by technical or operational evidence?
- Where is this mostly rhetorical compression rather than demonstrated divergence?

### Anti-Neocon / Anti-War-Establishment Blame

Macgregor frequently assigns responsibility to entrenched elite factions rather than to isolated tactical mistakes. That can help surface continuity of blame narratives across episodes.

Open review:

- Which blame patterns remain stable?
- How much of this family is voice-native versus host-elicited emphasis?

### War Termination by Exhaustion or Failure

Macgregor often frames war termination as forced by exhaustion, failed coercion, or the collapse of political room. This is the most directly comparable family to Davis's practical-room and Mearsheimer's bargaining geometry.

Open review:

- Where does Macgregor describe exhaustion as military, political, or narrative?
- Which claims align with settlement evidence already in the archive?

Use this claim map to route a Macgregor-relevant daily item:

1. Open [source-index.md](source-index.md) and the Daniel Davis channel shelf.

4. Compare against Mearsheimer when the claim concerns structural incentives or deterrence.
5. Compare against Marandi when the claim concerns Iranian endurance or red-line posture.
