## Core Frame

Kent repeatedly tests whether political promises about restraint, settlement, and counterterrorism can survive actual U.S.-Israel incentive structures.

What this voice tends to notice:

- Whether Israeli behavior can be restrained by U.S. aid, arms, or diplomatic leverage.
- Whether proxy-war logic is being repackaged as counterterrorism or peace enforcement.
- Whether personnel, intelligence, and donor pressures make stated policy unenforceable.

What this voice tends to emphasize:

- Practical enforcement levers over declaratory peace language.
- Israel-policy constraints inside Trump-world and U.S. security institutions.
- The danger of treating former adversarial networks as convenient regional proxies.

What this voice may miss or underweight:

- Non-U.S. diplomatic pathways that do not depend on Washington enforcing restraint.
- Economic or maritime mechanisms outside the counterterrorism/security lane.

## Use Guidance

Use this voice when:

- A source asks whether U.S. policy can actually restrain Israel or enforce an Iran/Hormuz settlement.
- Host framing invokes counterterrorism, donor-class pressure, proxy leverage, or institutional sabotage.

Be careful when:

- Kent is describing internal U.S. security or political pressure without independent verification.

Evidence needed before relying on this voice:
