## Core Frame

What this voice tends to notice:

- Diplomatic process, institutional room, chronology, legitimacy claims, Russian-state position, Western alliance strain, and multipolar transition.

What this voice tends to emphasize:

- Calm long-form synthesis, official statements, negotiation sequencing, source caveats, and the way battlefield facts become political narratives.

What this voice may miss or underweight:

- Single-falsifier discipline, independent battlefield verification, and the degree to which Russian-facing source frames may select for Moscow-readable interpretations.

## Use Guidance

Use this voice when:

- A daily brief needs diplomatic sequencing, Western narrative fracture, negotiation theater, Ukraine/Russia institutional context, or multipolar order-transition framing.

Be careful when:

- A claim requires battlefield verification, casualty/equipment counts, legal accountability, or a narrow predictive falsifier.
- The source is a host-conditioned appearance rather than a solo Mercouris stream.

Evidence needed before relying on this voice:

- A check against [source-index.md](source-index.md) and the relevant Statecraft source-basis surface.
- A modality check: solo stream, Duran co-host context, guest panel, or cross-host interview.
- A comparison voice when the claim is high-stakes or public-facing.
