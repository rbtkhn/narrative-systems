# Davis Claim Map

This map turns the Davis Statecraft source basis into an initial Narrative Geopolitics interpretive layer.

Davis adds the practical-room and military-feasibility axis: what force can still do, what coercion can no longer recover, what bargaining room is real, and where official rhetoric outruns implementation proof.

Status: `seed-map`

## Reading Rule

1. Treat Davis as a practical-room voice before treating him as a general anti-war commentator.
2. Separate Davis's own claims from the host pressure he applies to guests.
3. Preserve the difference between practical feasibility, Pape-style forecast mechanism, and Mearsheimer-style structural realism.
4. Add forecast IDs only after source-level evidence is imported or tightly bound.

## Claim Notes

### Military Feasibility

Davis's primary value is testing whether policy claims can survive force, terrain, logistics, capability, escalation cost, and implementation reality. He is not only asking whether a policy is desirable; he is asking whether it can work.

Open review:

- Which Davis sources best show the difference between practical feasibility and technical weapons verification?
- When should Davis be paired with Postol, Jermy, Ritter, or Martyanov before public synthesis?

### Coercive Limits

Davis is strong where coercion starts to outrun settlement logic. His question is often whether military pressure still has a political end-state or whether it has become self-defeating theater.

Open review:

- Which sources identify the point where pressure no longer produces usable bargaining room?
- How does this compare with Pape's coercive mechanism and Marandi's Iran-facing red-line claims?

### Negotiation Clock / One-Text Rule

Davis repeatedly pressures public claims about ceasefires, deals, and ultimatums with implementation checks: do the parties say the same thing, is there a public text, what is the timing, and what incentives make compliance plausible?

Open review:

- Which deal claims fail the one-text rule?
- Which clocks are negotiation clocks versus extension games?

### Evidence-Walk Discipline

Davis often uses a "show the evidence" method: walk through observable facts before accepting official optimism, media consensus, or hawkish strategic claims.

Open review:

- Which Davis evidence walks become durable source-review templates?
- Where does the method need outside technical or regional correction?

### Host-Conditioned Feasibility Testing

As host, Davis pulls guests toward practical war-termination, military feasibility, and end-state realism. This is valuable channel conditioning, but it should not be confused with the guest's own voice continuity.

Open review:

- Which Davis-hosted guest transformations deserve local channel shelves?
- When does Davis sharpen a guest, and when does he narrow the frame too much?

### Strategic Overreach / Restraint

Davis's restraint-realist background gives him a consistent sensitivity to overreach: finite U.S. military power, limited political objectives, and the danger of rhetoric exceeding capability.

Open review:

- Which overreach claims are practical/military versus structural/geopolitical?
- When should the claim be compared with Mearsheimer, Mercouris, or Diesen?

## Use In Daily Synthesis

Use this claim map to route a Davis-relevant daily item:

1. Identify whether Davis is acting as voice, host, writer, or social/source pointer.
2. Open [source-index.md](source-index.md) and the relevant upstream Statecraft surface.
3. Identify the relevant claim family.
4. Compare against Pape when the claim concerns mechanism, thresholds, or forecast review.
5. Compare against Mearsheimer when the claim concerns structural incentives or security-dilemma logic.
6. Compare against Mercouris when the claim concerns diplomatic sequence or institutional room.
7. Compare against Marandi when the claim concerns Iran-facing legitimacy, red lines, or resistance posture.
8. Compare against Diesen when the claim moves into order-transition consequence.
9. Import the source into `archive/sources/` before public-facing synthesis.
