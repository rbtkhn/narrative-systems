## Claim Notes

### Iranian Sovereignty Red Lines

Marandi is most valuable when the question is how Iranian actors frame acceptable and unacceptable outcomes. This is not the same as neutral verification; it is evidence of red-line language, legitimacy claims, and signaling posture.

Open review:

- Which red lines remain stable across hosts?
- Which red lines shift under live crisis pressure?

### Coercion Resistance / Failed Intimidation

Marandi often frames pressure as hardening rather than breaking Iran's position. This overlaps with Pape and Mearsheimer, but Marandi supplies the Iran-facing perception of why coercion fails.

Open review:

- Where does failed intimidation appear as Iranian signaling rather than external analysis?
- Which events would show coercion succeeding despite Marandi's frame?

### Selective Hormuz Leverage

Hormuz is not merely a transit object in Marandi's frame. It can become selective leverage, signaling, punishment, tolling, or passage control. This should be paired with external shipping, energy, and military verification before public use.

Open review:

- What distinction does Marandi make between full closure and selective passage?
- Which sources show concrete mechanism rather than rhetorical leverage?

### Gulf Complicity

Marandi can frame Gulf states as implicated when they enable U.S./Israeli pressure or blockade conditions. This is analytically important because it widens the political geography of accountability.

Open review:

- Which claims are warnings, accusations, or declared red lines?
- Which Gulf actors are differentiated rather than treated as a bloc?

### Negotiation Distrust and Conditional Diplomacy

Marandi's negotiation frame often treats talks as conditional, reversible, and vulnerable to sabotage. This is the regional-embedded counterpart to Pape's MOU fragility and Mercouris's negotiation theater.

Open review:

- What does Marandi count as proof that talks are sincere?
- Which host contexts intensify distrust language?

### Siege Endurance and Postwar Order

Marandi often links resilience under pressure to postwar regional order claims. This axis matters because it shows how Iran wants endurance to translate into legitimacy and regional status.

Open review:

- Where does endurance become a claim about future order rather than crisis survival?
- Which outside voices should pressure-test this transition?

## Use In Daily Synthesis

Use this claim map to route a Marandi-relevant daily item:

1. Identify whether the item came through Dialogue Works, Diesen, Davis, Napolitano, shorthand/X, or another support channel.
2. Open [source-index.md](source-index.md) and the relevant upstream Statecraft surface.
3. Identify the relevant claim family.
4. Compare against Pape when the claim concerns coercion, Hormuz clocks, or forecast mechanisms.
5. Compare against Mearsheimer when the claim concerns structure, deterrence, or bargaining geometry.
6. Compare against Mercouris when the claim concerns diplomatic sequence or institutional room.
7. Import the source into `archive/sources/` before public-facing synthesis.
