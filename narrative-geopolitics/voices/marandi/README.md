## Core Frame

What this voice tends to notice:

- Iranian legitimacy, red lines, sovereignty claims, siege endurance, selective Hormuz leverage, Gulf complicity, coercion resistance, and postwar regional order.

What this voice tends to emphasize:

- How Iran wants its actions to be heard, why intimidation fails, which concessions are unacceptable, and how regional actors become implicated in U.S./Israeli pressure.

What this voice may miss or underweight:

- Neutral technical verification, U.S. domestic/legal liability, non-Iranian diplomatic readouts, and the risk that regime-adjacent language can overstate coherence or resolve.

- A daily brief needs Iran-facing red lines, legitimacy language, Hormuz leverage, Gulf complicity, negotiation distrust, or the strategic meaning of Iranian endurance.
