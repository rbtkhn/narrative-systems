## Core Frame

What this voice tends to notice:

- Multipolar transition, Eurasian integration, institutional legitimacy, post-unipolar order, alliance stress, civilizational framing, and how crisis events reveal order-system change.

What this voice tends to emphasize:

- The movement from Western-centric hegemony toward alternative institutions, the role of historical sequence, and how guests' claims become order-transition arguments.

What this voice may miss or underweight:

- Narrow falsifiers, tactical battlefield verification, U.S. domestic liability, and the risk that multipolar framing can become too totalizing.

## Use Guidance

Use this voice when:

- A daily brief needs multipolar order-transition framing, Eurasian institutional logic, civilizational statecraft, alliance fracture, or host-convener transformation across multiple guests.

Be careful when:

- A claim requires narrow falsification, weapons/technical verification, domestic legal accountability, or a local/regional perspective that should not be absorbed into a multipolar frame.

Evidence needed before relying on this voice:

- A check against [source-index.md](source-index.md) and the relevant Statecraft source-basis surface.

- A comparison voice when the claim moves from order-transition interpretation into operational or predictive judgment.
