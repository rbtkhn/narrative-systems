## Claim Notes

### Security Dilemma

Mearsheimer's primary value is the state-level incentive map: expansion, threat perception, alliance pressure, and great-power counterpressure. Use this when an event needs structural location rather than personality or media-cycle explanation.

Open review:

- Which sources best distinguish security dilemma from ideological preference?
- When does the model underweight smaller-state agency?

### Limits of American Power

Mearsheimer often treats crisis outcomes as evidence that military and economic pressure cannot produce the promised political result. This axis is valuable because it turns defeat or stalemate into a structural constraint rather than a messaging failure.

Open review:

- Which limits are military, economic, diplomatic, or alliance-political?
- When does "limits of power" become too broad to falsify?

### Punishment Does Not Equal Surrender

This is the Mearsheimer bridge closest to Pape. Punishment may escalate costs without producing surrender, especially where core interests or regime survival are implicated.

Open review:

- Where does this overlap with Pape's smart-bomb trap?
- Where does Mearsheimer explain coercive failure structurally rather than operationally?

### Israel as Structural Liability

Mearsheimer can frame Israel as a strategic liability for the United States. This is analytically important but high-risk for public synthesis because it requires careful separation of structural argument, regional evidence, and host framing.

Open review:

- Which sources make the structural claim cleanly?
- Which regional voices should pressure-test the claim before public use?

### Negotiation Impossibility / No Offramp

Mearsheimer's bargaining geometry often emphasizes that settlement space narrows when interests harden, credibility costs mount, and coercive aims keep shifting. This should be compared with Mercouris's negotiation theater and Pape's MOU fragility.

Open review:

- What would count as an actual offramp rather than a rhetorical pause?
- Which host lane best preserves the bargaining logic?

### Order-Transition / NATO Decline

This family captures Mearsheimer's highest-altitude structural reading: NATO, Western power, multipolar adjustment, and great-power risk. It overlaps with Mercouris but differs by being more structural and less stream-sequential.

Open review:

- Where does order-transition become a theory rather than an observation?
- How should this be compared with Diesen if Diesen becomes a local voice?

## Use In Daily Synthesis

Use this claim map to route a Mearsheimer-relevant daily item:

1. Identify whether the item came through Diesen, Davis, Napolitano, Tucker, authored material, or a non-core appearance.
2. Open [source-index.md](source-index.md) and the relevant upstream Statecraft surface.
3. Identify the relevant claim family.
4. Compare against Pape when the claim is about coercive mechanism or forecast failure.
5. Compare against Mercouris when the claim is about diplomatic room, institutional sequence, or order transition.
6. Import the source into `archive/sources/` before public-facing synthesis.
