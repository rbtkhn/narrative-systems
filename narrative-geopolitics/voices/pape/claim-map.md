# Pape Claim Map

This map turns the Pape source routes into an interpretive retrieval layer.

Use it when the task is not just "find the source," but "understand the claim": what Pape appears to argue, where the claim is grounded, how it evolves, where interviews pressure it, and what still needs review.

Source truth remains in `../../archive/sources/`. Routing lives in [source-index.md](source-index.md), [authored-forecast-spine.md](authored-forecast-spine.md), and [guest-pressure-tests.md](guest-pressure-tests.md).

Status: `seed-map`

## Reading Rule

1. Treat authored sources as the claim baseline.
2. Treat guest appearances as pressure tests, amplifications, or public translations.
3. Do not promote a claim into daily synthesis without at least one source link and a modality check.
4. Add forecast IDs only when a claim has a reviewable prediction or falsifier.

## Claim Notes

### Smart-Bomb Trap

Pape's central mechanism is that precision coercion can create false confidence in bounded control. The authored spine should be used for the claim's disciplined form; interview appearances should be checked for urgency, simplification, or headline compression.

Open review:

- What concrete signals distinguish coercive failure from temporary resistance?
- Which events would falsify the claim by showing rapid concession without widening?

### Escalation Ledger

This is both a claim and a method. Pape's value is not only in warning that escalation may occur, but in tracking thresholds across deployments, chokepoints, diplomacy, markets, and regional actors.

Open review:

- Which ledger thresholds should become daily synthesis fields?
- Which thresholds are Pape-specific versus generally useful for Narrative Geopolitics?

### Blockade / Shortage Clock

This family turns supply pressure into a timing problem. It should be handled carefully because it depends on live facts, market behavior, and material shortage evidence.

Open review:

- Did Pape's shortage timing claims resolve into physical scarcity, price volatility, or political pressure?
- What external indicators should be checked before public use?

### Fourth-Center Thesis

This is the claim most likely to be misread. Preserve the narrow version: Iran as a coercive system-shaping center through leverage. Do not convert it into a comprehensive-power claim without separate evidence.

Open review:

- Where does Pape define "center" through leverage rather than broad power?
- How do interviews intensify the phrase compared with authored sources?

### Gulf Risk Premium / Energy Repricing

This family tracks whether formal reopening or de-escalation is less important than insurer, tanker, producer, and Gulf-state confidence. It is useful for public briefs only with fresh external verification.

Open review:

- Did prices compress after formal de-escalation, or did risk premiums persist?
- Which evidence best separates transient price movement from structural repricing?

### MOU / Deal Fragility

This family tracks whether diplomatic frameworks lower escalation pressure or merely create a temporary optics layer over unresolved leverage. It often appears in interview settings where host pressure can intensify the claim.

Open review:

- What would count as the MOU stabilizing rather than failing?
- Which host contexts push this claim toward headline certainty?

## Use In Daily Synthesis

Use this claim map to route a Pape-relevant daily item:

1. Identify the relevant claim family.
2. Open the baseline authored sources.
3. If the item came through a guest appearance, open [guest-pressure-tests.md](guest-pressure-tests.md) and the channel shelf.
4. Add a forecast/review hook if the claim is predictive.
5. Note whether the use is internal-only or safe for public summary.
