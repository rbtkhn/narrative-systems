# Ukraine Official-Terms Activation Baseline

As of: `2026-07-09`

Status: `defined-not-satisfied`

Purpose: define the minimum evidence needed to test whether a battlefield or
deep-strike threshold changes negotiating room. This is a one-crisis activation
check, not a new archive schema or intake requirement.

## Decision

The current archive does not establish an official-terms baseline. It contains
useful analyst-mediated descriptions of Russian, Ukrainian, and Western
positions, but no complete party-owned set of terms that can anchor a before
and after comparison.

Ukraine therefore remains a watch candidate. Additional commentary about who
is winning cannot satisfy this baseline.

## Minimum Activation Set

Promotion requires one threshold record and three actor snapshots.

### 1. Threshold Record

Name one bounded event that could plausibly change settlement terms:

- an adjudicated territorial-control change;
- a documented expansion or restriction of deep-strike authority;
- a new alliance force commitment or security guarantee; or
- the opening, suspension, or breakdown of an official negotiation.

The record must identify the date, the affected object, and the evidence basis.
A commentator's prediction or an unverified battlefield claim does not qualify.

### 2. Three Actor Snapshots

Capture a dated position from each of:

1. Russia;
2. Ukraine;
3. at least one external actor capable of supplying or guaranteeing the
   proposed settlement, such as the United States, NATO, the European Union, or
   a named European government.

Each snapshot must be party-owned: an official text, official readout, direct
speech or interview, published proposal, or other attributable primary record.
An archive transcript may point to that record, but analyst paraphrase alone
does not establish the party's terms.

### 3. Four Required Dimensions

Each actor snapshot must state a position or explicitly record `unknown` for:

| Dimension | Minimum question |
| --- | --- |
| Territory | What line, jurisdiction, recognition, or territorial disposition is demanded or accepted? |
| Alignment and guarantees | What NATO, neutrality, bilateral guarantee, or wider security arrangement is demanded or accepted? |
| Force posture | What demilitarization, rearmament, foreign-force, or deep-strike limits are demanded or accepted? |
| Sequence and implementation | Does fighting stop before or after political agreement, and who monitors or enforces compliance? |

Sanctions, frozen assets, elections, reconstruction, and legal recognition are
added only when a party makes them load-bearing. Missing dimensions remain
`unknown`; they are not inferred from a voice profile.

### 4. Before-and-After Rule

The latest qualifying pre-threshold snapshot supplies the baseline. After the
threshold, capture the first qualifying update from each actor or record that no
update appeared within the review window.

Compare each dimension as:

- `toward_overlap`;
- `unchanged`;
- `away_from_overlap`; or
- `unknown`.

The **conversion hypothesis** strengthens only when at least one load-bearing
dimension moves toward overlap without another moving away. The **hardening
hypothesis** strengthens when a load-bearing dimension moves away from overlap,
or when new force or alliance commitments make implementation more difficult.
If primary positions are absent, the result is `unresolvable`, not unchanged.

## Activation Rule

Re-run the Ukraine promotion test only when the archive contains:

- one qualifying threshold record;
- all three party-owned actor snapshots;
- coverage of the four required dimensions, with unknowns preserved; and
- enough before-and-after evidence to distinguish `toward_overlap`,
  `away_from_overlap`, and `unresolvable`.

Until then, continuity may improve interpretation and source discipline, but it
cannot support an active-dossier claim that military pressure is closing or
opening negotiating room. No probability-bearing forecast is authorized.
