## Comparative Value

The council adds two things beyond the baseline:

1. It distinguishes three competing accounts of what governs the transition:
   threshold propagation, bargaining closure, and failed reciprocity.
2. It upgrades the observable from challenge-plus-response to the shape and
   aftermath of the response: bounded return to bargaining versus cross-domain
   propagation without return.

These are material analytical gains, not merely additional attribution.

## Efficiency

Result: `unresolvable`.

The experiment was completed within one session, but the baseline workflow did
not preserve a comparable elapsed-time record. Claiming the treatment took no
more than twice as long would therefore be false precision. Future comparisons
must start and stop the same timer around control and treatment.

## Decision

`narrow` rather than `continue`.

The council clearly adds value for a deep Hormuz object with three developed
voices, but corpus depth is highly unequal (`75 / 25 / 16` routed sources), two
voices are dominated by hosted transcripts, and the efficiency criterion is
unresolved. The result authorizes another manual test with mature voices. It
does not authorize dialogue automation, generalization to thin crisis objects,
or public simulation of living people.

## Next Falsifier

Apply the same protocol to one question where the voices should *not* all be
useful. If the method forces three elaborate responses despite one or more
voices reaching `unknown`, it is generating council theater rather than
intellectual fidelity.
