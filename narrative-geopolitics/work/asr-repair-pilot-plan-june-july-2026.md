## Repair Scope For The Pilot

Allowed:

- proper noun correction
- repeated ASR collision cleanup
- obvious sentence break repair
- clear geopolitical entity normalization
- speaker-turn clarity where the intended speaker is obvious

Not allowed:

- paraphrase
- claim compression
- speculative wording repair
- silent strengthening of weak claims
- declaring the result verbatim-verified

## Measurement

Judge the pilot on these outcomes.

### 1. Section-label quality

Before:

- labels like `In A Bar On A`
- labels like `Policy For Even A Minute`
- labels like `Such They Started To Bomb`

After:

- labels surface actual topical turns
- fewer ASR-shaped headings survive

### 2. Synthesis speed

Measure whether the repaired files reduce time spent:

- inferring what a section is about
- scanning for the real substantive turn
- reconciling neighboring voices on the same object

### 3. Quote confidence

Measure whether a repaired file is easier to use for:

- claim extraction
- analyst quoting
- future public-draft validation

### 4. Retrieval quality

Measure whether search and browseability improve through:

- cleaner nouns
- cleaner section headings
- less filler-led phrase dominance

## Success Threshold

Scale the campaign only if Batch A shows at least two of these three:

- visibly better section labels across most files
- meaningfully faster synthesis use
- meaningfully higher quote confidence

If not, keep deeper repair narrow and demand-driven.

## Recommended Next Step

Operational sequence:

1. repair Batch A only
2. rerun sectioning on those repaired files
3. compare before/after headings and usability
4. write one short pilot findings note
5. decide whether Batch B is justified

## Why This Pilot Is High ROI

These files were chosen because they sit at the overlap of:

- recurring June-July strategic importance
- visible current section-label weakness
- likely reuse in synthesis or comparison

That makes them the best test of whether deeper ASR repair improves the system
rather than merely polishing the archive.
