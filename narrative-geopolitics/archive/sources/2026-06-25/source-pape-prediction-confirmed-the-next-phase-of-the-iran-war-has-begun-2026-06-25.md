# Prediction Confirmed: The Next Phase of the Iran War Has Begun

Prof Robert Pape

Jun 25, 2026

On Monday, I posted "Getting the Framework Right" identifying that one of the most likely escalation pathways in the Iran War would not be just about Lebanon.

It would be commercial shipping.

Specifically, I identified one of Iran's key red lines:

Any effort to interfere with Iran's emerging control over Hormuz would likely trigger renewed attacks on maritime traffic.

Today, reports of a merchant vessel being attacked near Oman suggest that this next phase may already be underway.

The important point is not simply that another ship was attacked.

The important point is that the strategic logic of escalation continues to unfold exactly as expected.

In Monday's analysis, I wrote:

Hormuz remains open because Iran permits it to remain open.

That sentence has become even more important today.

The Iran War and the "MOU peace" holding is not just about about Lebanon or nuclear facilities.

It is now also struggle over control of the world's most important energy chokepoint—and the global economy that depends on it.

Understanding where this war goes next requires understanding the logic driving these events, not simply reacting to today's headlines.

That's exactly what I try to do in The Escalation Trap.
