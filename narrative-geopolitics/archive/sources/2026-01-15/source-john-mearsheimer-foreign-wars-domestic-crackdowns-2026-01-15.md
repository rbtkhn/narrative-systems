# John Mearsheimer: Foreign Wars, Domestic Crackdowns

This January anchor gives the `Judging Freedom x Mearsheimer` lane its earliest current starting point on disk.

The central line is the link between external aggression and internal hardening. In this host lane Mearsheimer is being used to argue that foreign wars do not remain neatly external; they reshape domestic governance, weaken legal restraint, and normalize crackdowns at home. Napolitano intensifies that line by explicitly tying it to constitutional corruption and the collapse of institutional limits.

The second line is host transformation. Under Napolitano, Mearsheimer's realism becomes a more immediate critique of executive lawlessness: once the state treats its own will as higher than law abroad, it becomes easier to do the same domestically. That gives the Napolitano branch a distinct moral-political tone compared with Diesen and Davis, even when the strategic content overlaps.

The third line is continuity. This is a summary-grade routeable anchor, not a transcript-grade primary, but it materially opens the January side of the branch. It should be read together with the stronger February, March, and April Mearsheimer files now on disk.
