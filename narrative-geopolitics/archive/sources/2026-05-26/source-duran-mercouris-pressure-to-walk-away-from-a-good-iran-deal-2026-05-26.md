# The Duran / Alexander Mercouris - Pressure to Walk Away From a Good Iran Deal

**Alex Christoforou:** All right, Alexander, let's talk about the situation with Iran. We had a deal over the weekend, and now it looks like we may not have a deal, or maybe we will have a deal. What are your thoughts on all the back and forth that is happening?

**Alexander Mercouris:** Well, can I say I think it's very much the point that we have been making in program after program after program. There is in fact a deal on the table and objectively, and I do mean objectively in terms of what fulfills the core interests of the United States, it is actually a good deal in the sense that what has been suggested, what's going backwards and forwards, what Pakistan has been working on, and what was presented to Trump about ten days ago, a memorandum of understanding for an end to the current conflict, in other words the fighting, an end to the sea blockade, a reopening, a full reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, and a commitment by Iran to move forward with the question of nuclear enrichment and to do so within a specified period.

That suits American interests in my opinion at the present time, which are indeed to get the Strait of Hormuz reopened, to get the United States out of this war, which has not gone well and which is draining American resources and which is keeping a large part of the US fleet tied down trying to enforce an increasingly ineffective blockade.

And beyond that period of 30 to 60 days, there is a potential agreement on nuclear enrichment which is there on the table. It has arguably always been there on the table. Again, it accepts that the United States cannot get all of the things it was demanding before the war, in other words a total end to enrichment. But it does move the dial in ways that to some extent go beyond the JCPOA and which would ensure that Iran never acquires nuclear weapons, which it says it doesn't want.

So objectively we have a good deal there. At least this is my view. A deal which, had there not been a war, we could have validly said was a success. The problem is there has been a war. The problem also is that the United States went into that war with a totally different agenda, which is not to deal with nuclear enrichment, not to prevent the Iranians from acquiring a nuclear weapon.
