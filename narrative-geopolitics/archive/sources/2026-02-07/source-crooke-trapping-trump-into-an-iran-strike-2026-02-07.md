# trapping trump into an iran strike

strategy input crooke

https://conflictsforum.substack.com/p/trapping-trump-into-an-iran-strike Trapping Trump into an Iran strike? Netanyahu will deny an Iran deal its ‘kosher certificate’ if Iran’s missiles are omitted 
Alastair Crooke, 7 February 2026
Conflicts Forum
Feb 07, 2026

Netanyahu and his adherents see his Israeli hegemonic strategy to be in the process of ‘blowing up’: The state is falling into internal crisis, and he, like Trump, is getting desperate. He needs Trump not to just bomb Iran, but to remove it entirely from the strategic board through a bombing campaign in order to maintain the thrust behind the Greater Israel domination project.

To this end, Netanyahu has contrived the framework to an Iran trap for Trump — one consisting of the inversion of the nuclear issue as the main priority to be replaced by Iranian missiles as now being Israel’s primordial and existential threat. This was the message that Netanyahu delivered to Trump at Mar-a-Lago on 28 December 2025.

Israeli press reports are adamant that Trump at the Mar-a-Lago summit gave a ‘green light’ for a strike on Iran, led by the US. This is Israel’s narrative — but has not been confirmed by US sources.

The December 2025 summit resulted in the US engaging in the attempt to impose yet another deception on Iran in order to provide spurious validation for a major air and missile attack on Iran. Spurious -- as the US has known since the 2010 talks led by Iran’s negotiator at the time, Saeed Jalili, that Iran insists that its missile defence is non-negotiable (as one would expect of any sovereign nation).

Iran has made clear over the months since Trump launched the June 2025 attacks on its nuclear sites — which Trump proclaims “obliterated” Iran’s nuclear capabilities — that whilst Iran remains open to diplomacy on the technical conduct of its nuclear programme, Iran’s rights under the NPT — to enrich uranium for peaceful purposes — are not up for negotiation.

Last week, the US proposed another round of nuclear negotiations with Iran (Witkoff confirmed that the talks would be on nuclear issues only) as a backdrop to the dispatch by the US of a naval armada to the Persian Gulf and a build-up of munitions — in the pattern of the Venezuela naval blockade. Iran agreed to talks, but refused to conduct them under military threat.

The US did accede to Iran’s demands that the talks be held in Muscat and be limited to the nuclear file.

However, Marco Rubio then suddenly demanded that Iran limit the range of its ballistic missiles, “end” its nuclear program, and terminate its support for its regional allies.

Likely, this shift came through the intervention of Israel, which, from the time of the Mar-a-Lago meeting with Netanyahu in December 2025, has been insisting that the destruction of Iran’s missile inventory must take priority over a purely nuclear deal.

At the same time, those whom the US proposed to join the talks in Istanbul (Turkey, Egypt and Qatar) as “mediators” published their own framework for a possible US-Iran deal:

Iran would cease all enrichment on its soil for three years;

After three years, Iran would limit enrichment to 1.5%;

Iran’s current stock of 440kg of 60% enriched uranium would be transferred to a third country;

Iran would cease arming non-state actors in the region (alluding to Hamas, Hezbollah, Iraqi-based groups and Ansarullah);

Iran would agree to cease transferring technology to non-state actors in the region;

Iran would pledge not to use ballistic missiles;

The US and Iran to sign a non-aggression pact.

Iran flatly refused to shift the agreed locus from Muscat to Istanbul, and definitively rejected the US demand that its ballistic missiles and its support for its allies, such as Hamas and Hezbollah, be on the agenda. The Rubio ploy was transparent — the ‘mediators’ were being included to exert pressure on Iran to accept what amounted to an Israeli agenda.

The US reaction to this Iranian refusal was an ultimatum: Agree or there would be no negotiations.

Iran retorted: “Ok, then nothing”

US officials were surprised by Iran’s reaction: “We didn’t expect the Iranians to say ‘no’”, a US official told Reuters. Some in Washington apparently expected Iran to show up in Istanbul with a ‘surrender document’.

Within hours the US retreated and accepted Iran’s position that the Friday meeting in Oman would only address nuclear weapons and uranium enrichment.

Israel was not happy: Israeli military correspondent for Channel 14 (and a leading Netanyahu ‘spokesman’), Yaakov Bardugo, threatened that Israel will take unilateral action if the US did not address Israel’s red-lines in negotiations. Bardugo claims too that Israel has received the green light from Trump for military action against Iran’s ballistic missile program:

“We will not leave ballistic missiles in Iran’s hands. Ballistic missiles are an existential threat no less than the Iranian nuclear program. It’s possible, as in the Twelve-Day War or in other cases, that Israel will take the first steps to war. If the US fails to address Israel’s red-lines, Tel Aviv will retain the right to attack on the issue of ballistic missiles, and as far as I understand, the approval to strike ballistic missiles was already given in Mar-a-Lago”.

Let us be clear -- this is a trap for Trump.

“Tuesday’s visit by American envoy Steve Witkoff did not soothe the Israeli political and security leadership’s fears that the US might agree to a partial deal with Iran”, Ben Caspit wrote on 6 February. “Israel is concerned that Washington will accept a partial deal that focuses only on the nuclear issue, ignoring Iran’s ballistic missile project and support for its proxies”.

In Israel, “war with Iran [is indeed] on the agenda”, notes Anna Barsky in Ma’ariv. “The big question is whether the Iranians are willing to give up their honour and their principles and show complete surrender: None of this will probably happen … [But] unlike in other arenas, a [partial] deal with Iran will not pass in Washington without the Israeli “Kosher Certificate”. Not because of a formal veto, but because Netanyahu is considered by the Republican establishment to be a symbol of the hard line against Iran”.

“For Trump, it is difficult to produce a ‘victory image’ against Tehran [i.e. a partial nuclear deal] if Jerusalem refuses to join the story; or worse -- attacks it​ …”.

There you have it — the Israeli security establishment is cornering Trump into a war he likely would prefer to avoid. Iran will stand firm on its principles in respect to enrichment and its missiles.

On the other hand, a chorus of retired US generals are promoting the cult of American military invincibility which will have no problem to collapse Iranian state structures in a prolonged aerial bombardment campaign. As former Vice Chief-of-Staff of the US Army, General Jack Keane, told Fox Business news on 2 Feb:

“We have a clear and historic choice … Let’s assume we get a good deal [with Iran] — no enrichment … [which means] they can’t produce a nuclear weapon. Let’s say we even get further than that — no or limited ballistic missiles … and no support for proxies. Let’s assume we get that far … [A deal just] extends the life of this regime indefinitely … We have a major paradigm shift in the Middle East … not [seen] since the Islamic [Revolution] 45 years ago … [Now] we can take them off the board finally … And who will achieve that? President Trump and PM Netanyahu, and without Trump’s involvement here, this [won’t] happen. I’ve got to believe that the President understands this clearly … I’ve got to have some confidence that he’s going to take these guys off the board …”.

Can Trump find a path to reverse out from this impasse that he has largely created for himself (when he did not challenge Netanyahu’s prioritising of the missile threat)? Will his big pro-Israeli donors permit him to back away? Can nuclear talks be spun out endlessly in the face of the massive public propaganda tsunami being mounted in the western (and Israeli) press of Iran “butchering” its own people?

Netanyahu threatens to act unilaterally against Iran, although this is probably bluster. Israel can do nothing of that nature without US support.

We are faced with two desperate and increasingly erratic leaders. Might they act irrationally – and self-destructively? Two known ‘unknowns’.

The fight Israel is having with the US, writes Anna Barsky, is a “fight over [the] agenda” regarding Iran’s missile programme. Israel, she says, “has ... been concentrating on an effort to influence [the US on the missile issue] so as to make [it] an integral part of every American decision, whether military or political”.

“This is the gap that keeps Israel awake at night … [Missiles] are the immediate threat, the weapon that [gives] Iran ... a strategic umbrella ... to exert deterrence even without crossing the threshold … The Iranian position on [this] issue is tough … When it comes to missiles, the door is locked. From its perspective, this is its sovereign capability, a tool that balances the air superiority of the US and Israel, and above all, an asset that allows it to continue to manage the regional struggle without being drawn into direct conflict”.
