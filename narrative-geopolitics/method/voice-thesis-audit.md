# Voice Thesis Audit

Use this method when a voice’s prediction or analytical thesis needs to be traced over time and pressure-tested against other voices.

## Core Rule

Audit the thesis in layers:

```text
thesis statement → mechanism → predicted outcome → falsifier
```

Do not treat repeated statements by the same voice as independent corroboration.

## Workflow

1. **Define the thesis narrowly.** Separate the object from surrounding commentary.
2. **Trace the voice chronologically.** Record the first formulation, revisions, timing changes, and current form.
3. **Separate mechanism from outcome.** A thesis may correctly identify a mechanism while missing its predicted result.
4. **Audit other voices.** Classify each as:
   - direct disagreement;
   - conditional divergence;
   - mechanism disagreement;
   - timing divergence;
   - reinforcement;
   - non-engagement.
5. **State the falsifier.** Identify an observable development that would weaken or disprove the thesis.
6. **Close with unresolved seams.** Distinguish missing evidence, source dependence, timing ambiguity, and genuine disagreement.

## Required Output

- core thesis;
- chronological trace;
- thesis evolution;
- pressure-test matrix;
- direct disagreements;
- assessment of strongest and weakest links;
- separate review questions for mechanism and outcome.

## Permanent Guardrails

- Longitudinal continuity is not independent corroboration.
- A forecast can be directionally right while its mechanism or timing is wrong.
- Continued activity is not automatically confirmation; it must match the thesis’s predicted mechanism.
- Attribute source claims and do not promote them to operational fact without verification.
- If no direct disagreement exists, say so; do not manufacture one from a different emphasis.
