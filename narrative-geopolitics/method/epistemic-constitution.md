# Epistemic Constitution

Version: `0.1`

Status: `provisional cross-surface method contract`

Review trigger: after five additional substantive manifest-backed daily issues, and no later than `2026-08-15`.

## Purpose and Authority

This constitution governs how consequential claims carry epistemic posture across Narrative Geopolitics. It exists to preserve continuity without allowing repetition, presentation, or automation to manufacture warrant.

The [Reality Verification Lattice](reality-verification-lattice.md) implements
this law for migrated claims through typed records, multilingual corroboration,
human signoff, and append-only transitions.

It is deliberately subordinate in matters of authority. The [bounded-agency contract](bounded-agency-contract.md) determines who may act and which surfaces may change. The archive, [verification](../work/verification/README.md), forecast, [daily-issue](daily-issue.md), and publication contracts determine what counts as admissible support in their domains. This document supplies a common law for transitions between those domains. If it conflicts with a more specific canonical contract, the specific contract controls and the conflict should be recorded for this constitution's next review.

## Governing Law

> Every consequential claim carries its epistemic state, provenance, scope, dependencies, and transition history into each downstream use; no downstream repetition upgrades that state.

Epistemic posture may resonate downstream. Evidentiary warrant may advance only through accountable upstream support.

A **consequential claim** is one whose adoption could materially change synthesis, forecast accountability, operational assessment, public language, or operator action. An **epistemic state** describes the claim's warranted use, not its ultimate truth. **Upstream support** is evidence or authorized assessment admitted by the controlling domain contract. A **downstream use** is any synthesis, forecast, verification request, issue, public artifact, agent handoff, or decision that depends on the claim.

## Six Changes That Must Remain Distinct

| Change | Meaning | Does not by itself mean |
| --- | --- | --- |
| World | The underlying event or condition changed. | The system has observed it. |
| Evidence | Admissible information became available, disappeared, or changed quality. | The interpretation must change. |
| Interpretation | The mechanism, implication, or best explanation changed. | New evidence arrived. |
| Confidence boundary | The warranted strength or scope of judgment changed. | The claim became operational fact. |
| Authorization | A governed actor or contract permitted a new use. | The evidence became stronger. |
| Presentation | Wording, placement, format, or audience changed. | Any preceding state changed. |

These changes may occur together, but each transition must name its actual cause. A generated issue is normally a presentation change. A completed verification packet may support an authorization or confidence change. Neither event silently proves that the world changed.

## Constitutional Invariants

1. **Provenance travels.** A consequential claim never loses its originating source chain, bounded scope, or material caveats when reused.
2. **Derivation is directional.** Dependencies point from downstream judgment toward upstream support. A derivative artifact cannot return as independent corroboration of its own ancestors.
3. **Recurrence is not independence.** Multiple voices, URLs, agents, or publications inheriting one originating report remain one evidence chain.
4. **States describe permitted use.** Labels such as `source_assertion`, `operationally_supported`, `contested`, or `open` do not declare permanent truth and cannot outrank their controlling records.
5. **Transitions name causes and authority.** Every consequential upgrade, downgrade, retirement, or reopening identifies what changed, which surface supports it, and who or what contract authorized the use.
6. **Uncertainty remains available.** `Unresolved`, `mixed`, `unresolvable_with_authorized_evidence`, hold, retirement, and reopening are valid outcomes. Prose must not repair missing evidence.
7. **Presentation cannot promote itself.** Readability, repetition, headline placement, generated-file status, and public distribution never increase evidentiary warrant.
8. **Automation may route; governed judgment adopts.** Deterministic tools may detect staleness, trace dependencies, propose transitions, and block invalid states. They do not make semantic assessments reserved to a human or named contract.

## Transition Authority

| Operation | Automation may | Governed authority must |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Intake | Preserve supplied material, detect duplicates, and append a valid manifest row. | Resolve ambiguous identity, provenance, or source meaning. |
| Synthesis | Trace source IDs, validate labels, and surface dependencies. | Adopt an interpretation, select a story, and bound uncertainty. |
| Forecast | Validate hook identity, dates, bands, and ledger consistency. | Author a wager or determine its review outcome. |
| Verification | Collect within named observables and validate packet completeness. | Assess support, contestation, disconfirmation, or irresolvability. |
| Issue rendering | Render selected canonical inputs and reject drift. | Authorize factual posture in canonical copy. |
| Publication | Check gates, links, dates, and revision state. | Intentionally promote the artifact and accept its public claim boundary. |

Agents may propose a transition and identify its consequences. They may execute it only when their current mandate and the controlling domain contract authorize the mutation.

## Examples

**Verification changes a dependent issue.** A source asserts that a bypass route is operating. Synthesis records the assertion and opens a verification request. An assessed packet later contests the operating claim. That evidence transition may weaken confidence, keep a forecast open, and require conditional issue language. The issue, forecast note, and repeated agent summaries remain consequences of the packet; they do not become additional evidence against the route.

**Publication changes presentation only.** Canonical daily files generate an issue and an operator later promotes a bounded public brief. The audience, title, and prose change. Unless new admissible support entered through an authorized surface, the claim's evidence and confidence states remain unchanged.

## Failure Cases

**Synthetic corroboration.** Three agents cite a generated issue that cites one source assertion. A later synthesis counts the three agent outputs as convergence. This violates provenance travel, directional derivation, and source independence.

**Authorization laundering.** A human approves publication of carefully attributed language. A later agent treats that approval as verification of the underlying event. This collapses authorization into evidence and must be rejected.
