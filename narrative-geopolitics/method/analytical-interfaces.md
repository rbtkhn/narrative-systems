# Labels as Analytical Interfaces

Status: `active method contract`

## Principle

Every analytical label should help the next reader recover the judgment. A label should name the tension, mechanism, asymmetry, threshold, or change that makes its object analytically distinct—not merely the topic or container.

This principle governs crisis objects, lead judgments, forecasts, watch objects, voice roles, dialogue questions, uncertainty labels, and reader-facing section headings. It extends the [editorial title contract](editorial-titles.md).

## Operating Tests

### Crisis objects

A crisis object is a contested relationship, not a geographic noun.

- Thin: `Hormuz`
- Analytical: `Who governs commercially tolerable passage through Hormuz?`

The object must be bounded enough that evidence can change its state.

### Lead judgments

A lead judgment states what changed, why it matters, and the governing mechanism or asymmetry. It must not begin as a source summary. If the sentence could describe several unrelated days, rewrite it.

### Forecasts

A forecast is a causal wager, not topic plus outcome. It must specify:

- the observable claim;
- the mechanism expected to produce it;
- the time boundary;
- strengthening and weakening evidence;
- resolution criteria;
- the principal alternative explanation.

### Watch objects

Name the transition or threshold under observation. Stable geographic or conflict labels may appear as search aliases, but the displayed object should explain what the system is waiting to learn.

### Voice roles

Describe a recurring intellectual operation with a verb phrase. Prefer `tests political objectives against force capacity` to `military voice`. A role constrains retrieval; it does not define a personality.

### Dialogue questions

A council question must contain a disputed premise, tradeoff, or mechanism capable of producing differentiated answers. `What does the council think about Iran?` is a topic request, not an analytical question.

### Uncertainty

Every uncertainty label should name its source:

- `unknown—no operational verification`;
- `contested—shared evidence, divergent interpretation`;
- `uncertain—intent inferred from deployment`;
- `unresolved—timing prevents ex-ante credit`.

### Section headings

In reader-facing analysis, headings should expose the reasoning chain. `Background`, `Analysis`, `Discussion`, `Conclusion`, and `What Happens Next` are drafting scaffolds. Replace them with claim-bearing headings unless a functional heading such as `Sources` or `Evidence Status` is genuinely clearer.

## Required Drafting Pass

Before completing synthesis or publication:

1. Underline every label that merely names a topic or container.
2. Replace it with the contested relationship or analytical operation.
3. Check that the lead judgment and forecast expose mechanisms rather than consensus.
4. Attach uncertainty to its evidentiary cause.
5. Preserve stable IDs, filenames, and source identities separately from display language.

## Immutable Boundary

Do not apply analytical retitling to archive source titles, quotations, manifest identities, canonical slugs, hook IDs, CLI names, or provenance classifications. Those surfaces optimize for fidelity and stability. Analytical interfaces optimize for meaning.

## Automation Boundary

Repository validation rejects generic drafting headings in documents governed by the reader-facing title contract. Templates and tests preserve the required fields for crisis objects, forecasts, dialogue questions, uncertainty causes, and voice operations. Human review remains responsible for whether a label is genuinely discriminating.
