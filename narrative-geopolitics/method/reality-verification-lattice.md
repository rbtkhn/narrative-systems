# Reality Requires More Than Agreement

Status: `active-v1 method contract`

## Purpose

The Reality Verification Lattice is the canonical adjudication layer for
claims explicitly migrated into `work/reality/`. It does not replace the
archive. The archive establishes what a source said; the lattice records what
bounded claims later evidence can responsibly support, contest, disconfirm, or
leave unresolved.

The lattice is a graph rather than a source whitelist. Sources, evidence,
claims, observables, investigations, assessments, relations, and epistemic
transitions retain separate identities. Human assessments become authoritative
only inside the bounded use their evidence supports.

## Multilingual Agreement

Positive empirical support requires agreement on the same observable across
independent original-language environments and independent evidence chains:

- ordinary claims require two languages and two chains;
- high-consequence claims require three languages and three chains;
- one environment must be affected-region or claimant-situated;
- one must be external or challenged;
- the third high-consequence environment must be independent of both.

The original language and the language reviewed are recorded separately. An
official English edition of a Chinese statement remains Chinese-origin
evidence. Ten translations of one statement remain one language environment
and one chain.

Agreement is not a vote among narratives. Sources must converge on the same
bounded event, measurement, document, or actor position. When English,
Chinese, Russian, Arabic, Persian, or other environments disagree, the system
records the disagreement and preserves `contested` rather than choosing the
largest coalition.

Sensor, registry, imagery, tracking, and commercial records can anchor the
physical question without having a language. They strengthen an assessment but
do not normally replace multilingual coverage. A language waiver requires
unusually strong primary physical evidence, a documented search for the
missing environment, and two distinct human reviewers. Waived assessments are
visibly marked and excluded from headline voice-calibration rates.

## Typed Claims

Operational facts and forecasts may receive empirical outcomes. Causal claims
are assessed only relative to named alternatives. Interpretations may be
retained, narrowed, or superseded while their factual dependencies remain
separate. Actor-position claims establish what an institution said, not whether
its statement was true. Normative claims remain outside empirical scoring.

Compound investigations may group related questions, but each atomic claim
receives its own assessment. Supporting one incident does not automatically
support attribution, intent, warning sequence, or causal interpretation.

## Authority and Transition

Automation may create scaffolds, validate schemas and provenance, calculate
language and chain coverage, render views, and report downstream impact. It
never signs an assessment.

One human may assess an ordinary claim. High-consequence public adoption or
accountable forecast scoring requires two distinct signoffs. A one-reviewer
high-consequence result remains `provisional_assessed` and may guide internal
research without authorizing factual publication or forecast resolution.

Every consequential change creates an append-only transition distinguishing
world, evidence, interpretation, confidence, authorization, and presentation.
Generated issues and downstream summaries may carry the result, but they can
never return as new evidence for their own upstream claims.

## Compatibility

Existing `OPC-*`, `NG-*`, `VER-*`, and `VSRC-*` identities are preserved. When
a claim exists in the lattice, structured state controls and its Markdown
surfaces are generated or checked. Unmigrated historical claims continue under
the legacy verification contract until explicitly migrated.

For daily `OPC-*` rows that need a legacy verification packet, prefer the
guided attach command:

```powershell
.\scripts\python.ps1 scripts\verification.py attach --date YYYY-MM-DD --claim OPC-YYYYMMDD-NN --slug bounded-claim-label
```

`attach` creates or reuses a requested `VER-*` packet, links it from the
canonical synthesis row, and refreshes generated issue output when present. It
does not investigate evidence, admit sources, assess the claim, authorize
publication, or score a forecast.

The July 10 and July 14 pilots establish the boundary. July 10 preserves its
historical assessed packet while decomposing incident occurrence, attribution,
bypass intent, warnings, and causality. July 14 records three unresolved
operational investigations, an interpretation transition, and a presentation
transition without upgrading evidence or forecast probability.

## Voice Calibration

The lattice never produces a universal credibility score. Internal calibration
appears only after ten adjudicated empirical claims of the same type and domain
across at least three crisis objects. It reports denominators, outcomes,
language coverage, forecast-band behavior, scope discipline, update behavior,
source hygiene, and falsifier quality. Public voice profiles are outside v1.
