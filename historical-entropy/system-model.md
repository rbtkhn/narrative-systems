# Historical Entropy System Model

## System Class

Historical Entropy is a governed historical inheritance system.

Its primary job is to preserve the lineage and transformation history of
inherited objects without mistaking continuity for sameness, recurrence for
independent corroboration, or narrative coherence for evidentiary warrant.

Its delivery form is a cumulative public lecture series companion to
[Predictive History](https://github.com/rbtkhn/predictive-history). Predictive
History provides routes through durable historical sources; Historical Entropy
asks what those and independently verified sources can support about survival,
transformation, and loss. Neither project supplies evidence merely by being
linked from the other.

## Layer Model

```text
source floor
-> attributable observation
-> carrier
-> transmission claim
-> transformation assessment
   -> preservation
   -> compression
   -> mutation
   -> loss
   -> recovery
-> rival lineage or rupture check
-> bounded inheritance model
-> later reactivation
-> review against original evidence
```

## State Dimensions

Every consequential object should keep the following dimensions distinct:

| Dimension | Governing question |
| --- | --- |
| Evidence | What upstream material supports the object? |
| Lineage | Through which carriers and transformations did it travel? |
| Content | What remained, changed, or disappeared? |
| Context | Which original conditions still apply? |
| Capacity | Can an institution or community still enact the inheritance? |
| Contestation | Which rival lineages, ruptures, or exclusions remain? |
| Activation | Who is using the inheritance now, for what purpose? |
| Authorization | What downstream use is permitted? |

A change in one dimension does not silently change another. Public repetition
may increase activation while leaving evidence unchanged. Institutional revival
may increase capacity without proving the claimed lineage. New sources may
change evidence without restoring a lost practice.

## Invariants

1. **Provenance survives transmission.** Every derived object points toward its
   upstream sources and material caveats.
2. **Derivation remains directional.** A downstream synthesis cannot return as
   independent support for its own ancestors.
3. **Continuity is a claim.** Similarity, recurrence, and self-identification do
   not alone establish an unbroken lineage.
4. **Rupture remains representable.** The system permits discontinuity,
   reinvention, recovery, and irresolvable lineage.
5. **Civilizations are lenses, not containers.** A world object may participate
   in several civilizational accounts or in none.
6. **Entropy is multidimensional.** No universal decay score or single arrow of
   historical disorder is permitted.
7. **Patterns remain bounded.** Every pattern carries scope, counterexamples,
   and conditions under which it should not be retrieved.
8. **Present use requires present evidence.** Historical inheritance may frame
   a live question; it does not verify a live-world claim.
9. **The manuscript precedes the recording.** The reviewed manuscript is
   canonical. A transcript is derivative and identifies material departures.
10. **Candidate queues do not confer warrant.** CMC candidates remain leads
    until independently resolved; its internal authority labels do not cross
    the project boundary.

## Lecture Packet Contract

Completed lectures use stable IDs `he-01` through `he-08` and contain five
non-duplicative artifacts:

1. a doorway README for reading order, outcomes, status, and guardrails;
2. a speakable 2,000–3,000 word manuscript;
3. orientation YAML for identity, sequence, mechanism, cases, paths, review,
   and public readiness;
4. a source dossier for claim locations, source review, candidate conversion,
   exclusions, and independence notes;
5. commentary for the neutral summary, claim map, rival readings, entropy
   assessment, limits, and reader-value test.

Orientation YAML is authoritative for mechanical status and paths. Duplicated
prose in the doorway and commentary is explanatory; it does not create a
second mechanical authority. Semantic packet automation remains deferred
during the pilot.

Consequential claims receive packet-local IDs in the dossier and commentary.
They stay out of spoken prose unless a footnote materially helps the reader.
External sibling-project links are ordinary doorway links for navigation and
commit-pinned links when they participate in evidence or candidate conversion.

## Object Lifecycle

```text
proposed
-> source-linked
-> lineage-bounded
-> contested or provisionally supported
-> available for retrieval
-> revised, held, retired, or reopened
```

No object becomes authoritative merely because it has been named, formatted,
reused, or promoted. Later review may narrow or retire it without deleting its
history.

## Contrast With Predictive History

Predictive History begins from durable historical source material and builds a
guided reading route.

Historical Entropy begins from an attributable observation and asks what
survived transmission, what changed, what disappeared, and which later claims
of continuity are warranted.

Predictive History helps a reader encounter the past. Historical Entropy helps
a reader audit what later worlds carried forward from it.

## Contrast With Narrative Geopolitics

Narrative Geopolitics begins from live source intake and builds accountable
present judgment.

Historical Entropy may supply a prior, analogy candidate, or inheritance
question. Narrative Geopolitics remains responsible for current evidence,
actor assessment, uncertainty, forecast hooks, and public authorization.

## Pilot Value Test

Before creating a corpus, select one object that meets all of these conditions:

- it has a recoverable historical source chain;
- it passed through at least two materially different carriers or contexts;
- something important was preserved and something important changed;
- a rival continuity claim or rupture is available;
- the inheritance is invoked in a present geopolitical or public-historical
  setting;
- the present claim can be evaluated without treating the inheritance model as
  new evidence.

The pilot succeeds only if a reader can encounter the lecture without the
apparatus and can use the apparatus to explain what became more ordered, what
became less recoverable, why transmission is not uninterrupted continuity, the
principal evidence chain and counter-reading, and why the lecture is an
interpretation rather than new evidence.
