# Historical Entropy

Historical Entropy is a governed long-memory system for studying what happens
to knowledge, institutions, identities, practices, and narratives as they pass
through time.

It traces preservation, compression, mutation, loss, recovery, and later
reactivation. It does not treat history as inevitably decaying, assign a
thermodynamic score to civilizations, or convert recurring patterns into laws.

## System Class

```text
historical-entropy = governed historical inheritance system
predictive-history = public historical corpus traversal system
narrative-geopolitics = live geopolitical judgment system
```

Its central object is not a civilization imagined as a single mind. It is a
traceable transmission: something carried from an upstream source or practice
into a later setting, possibly changed along the way.

## Governing Definition

Historical entropy is the loss of recoverable distinctions, provenance,
context, and institutional capacity during transmission through time.

The definition is diagnostic, not thermodynamic. Entropy may increase in one
dimension while order increases in another. A state may standardize law while
destroying local memory. A doctrine may become more coherent while losing the
conditions and evidence that originally bounded it.

## Purpose

The project should help a reader or model recover:

- what was transmitted;
- which carrier transmitted it;
- what evidence supports the transmission claim;
- what was preserved, compressed, mutated, or lost;
- which later actors reactivated or reconstructed it;
- which rival inheritances or discontinuities remain available;
- how the inherited object may condition present judgment without becoming
  evidence for a current claim.

## Epistemic Boundary

Historical Entropy may organize and interpret source-grounded material. It may
not manufacture additional warrant through repetition, synthesis, naming, or
downstream reuse.

> A derived inheritance object never becomes independent evidence for the
> source claims from which it descends.

When a Historical Entropy object informs Predictive History or Narrative
Geopolitics, the downstream artifact must be able to return to the original
source chain. Several interpretations descending from one source remain one
evidence lineage.

## Initial Object Vocabulary

- **Source:** the upstream evidence floor.
- **Carrier:** the person, institution, text, ritual, technology, or practice
  through which something travels.
- **Transmission:** a bounded claim that an object passed between contexts.
- **Preservation:** material distinctions remain recoverable.
- **Compression:** complexity is reduced while some usable structure remains.
- **Mutation:** the inherited object changes in a way material to later use.
- **Loss:** a distinction, provenance chain, capacity, or context becomes
  unavailable.
- **Recovery:** later evidence or reconstruction restores recoverable context.
- **Reactivation:** an inherited object is made operative in a new setting.
- **Rival lineage:** a competing account of origin, continuity, or meaning.
- **Rupture:** continuity is not supported across a material boundary.

These are analytical interfaces, not declarations of historical truth.

## Relationship to the Sibling Projects

Predictive History owns guided traversal of durable historical sources.
Historical Entropy may learn from those routes, but it does not replace their
source floor.

Narrative Geopolitics owns live intake, claim verification, synthesis,
forecasting, and public geopolitical judgment. Historical Entropy may provide
historical priors or inheritance questions, but live claims must be supported
and adjudicated on the Narrative Geopolitics side.

```text
Predictive History source route
-> attributable historical observation
-> Historical Entropy transmission object
-> bounded inheritance model
-> optional retrieval by Narrative Geopolitics
-> return to original evidence before consequential use
```

## Public Form

Historical Entropy is an original public lecture series. Each lecture must work
without its apparatus, while every consequential historical claim remains
recoverable through a five-part packet: doorway, manuscript, orientation,
source dossier, and commentary. Manuscripts target 2,000–3,000 words and are
canonical before recording; a later transcript is derivative and must record
material departures.

For mechanical packet status and paths, orientation YAML is authoritative.
Doorway and commentary prose explain that state but do not override it.
Semantic packet automation remains deferred during the pilot.

Volume I is intentionally Eurasian. Its boundary is an editorial scope, not a
claim that other regions or oral and material carriers are less important.
Those require a later volume with source practices strong enough for them.

## Candidate Queue Boundary

The [Civilization Memory repository](https://github.com/rbtkhn/civilization_memory)
may propose cases and claims. It is never historical evidence. Every candidate
is recorded with a commit, path, extracted claim, and disposition; admission
requires independent resolution to a primary, near-primary, material, or
scholarly source with a stable location. Its naming, canon, doctrine, and
author-admissibility rules do not transfer here.
