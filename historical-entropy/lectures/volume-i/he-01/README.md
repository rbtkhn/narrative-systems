## Learning Outcomes

After the lecture, a reader should be able to:

- explain what Qin standardization made more legible to an imperial state;
- identify local distinctions and operating contexts that became harder to
  recover;
- distinguish a transmitted administrative repertoire from unchanged
  institutional continuity;
- reconstruct the main evidence chain and the strongest counter-reading;
- explain why “historical entropy” is this lecture's interpretation, not a new
  piece of historical evidence.

## Guardrails

- Entropy does not mean inevitable decline, moral degeneration, or a numerical
  score.
- “Qin” and “Han” do not name unitary actors or perfectly implemented systems.
- Excavated documents, retrospective histories, and modern scholarship answer
  different questions and are not interchangeable.
- CMC supplied search candidates only. Its authority labels and quotations are
  not evidence here.
- The manuscript is canonical before recording. Any transcript must remain
  derivative and log material departures.
