# Historical Entropy — Volume I

Volume I is an eight-lecture Eurasian sequence about simultaneous ordering and
loss. Each lecture is independently readable; together they teach a reader to
identify carriers, separate continuity from sameness, recognize context loss,
compare rival lineages, and resist unsupported decline claims.

The boundary is deliberate. Oral-memory and materially led cases belong in a
later volume after the project develops adequate methods, partnerships, and
source conventions.

## Sequence and Decision-Complete Briefs

### he-01 — When Order Becomes Legible

- **Doorway question:** What can a state make easier to see while making other
  distinctions harder to recover?
- **Mechanism:** standardization through script, measures, document forms, and
  administrative categories.
- **Case:** Qin unification and early Han adaptation.
- **Carrier tension:** state documents and retrospective histories preserve the
  imperial project while filtering local variation and uneven practice.
- **Reader outcome:** explain historical entropy through a concrete case without
  equating it with decline.
- **Feasibility:** exemplar complete; non-public pending review.
- **Packet:** [read he-01](he-01/README.md).

### he-02 — How to Read What Survived

- **Doorway question:** What must be shown before calling an inheritance
  continuous?
- **Mechanism:** carrier, transmission, loss, mutation, rupture, and rival
  lineage as a reusable reading method.
- **Cases:** bounded returns to he-01 plus short demonstrations selected only
  from independently verified sources.
- **Carrier tension:** a surviving text is not a surviving context or capacity.
- **Reader outcome:** audit a transmission claim without demanding either pure
  continuity or total rupture.
- **Feasibility:** ready for outline after he-01 review; method depends on the
  pilot vocabulary earning its maintenance cost.
- **Exclusion:** no universal entropy score or mandatory ontology.

### he-03 — Law After Empire

- **Doorway question:** How can legal preservation depend on radical
  compression and later reconstruction?
- **Mechanism:** selection, codification, institutional copying, and reuse.
- **Cases:** Justinianic compilation and later Roman-law reception.
- **Carrier tension:** the corpus preserves law by selecting, editing, and
  reorganizing earlier juristic material.
- **Reader outcome:** distinguish textual survival, institutional continuity,
  and later reactivation.
- **Feasibility:** ready for source assembly; use the *Corpus Juris Civilis*,
  manuscript history, and modern legal-history scholarship before prose.
- **Exclusion:** no claim that modern civil law is unchanged Roman law.

### he-04 — Baghdad Translates the World

- **Doorway question:** When translation preserves knowledge, what does it
  necessarily transform?
- **Mechanism:** multilingual selection, translation, commentary, patronage,
  and disciplinary reclassification.
- **Cases:** Greek, Persian, and Indian materials in Arabic scholarly settings.
- **Carrier tension:** “translation movement” is a retrospective aggregate, not
  a single pipeline or one civilizational handoff.
- **Reader outcome:** identify transformation as a condition of preservation.
- **Feasibility:** ready for source assembly if narrowed to two traceable works
  and their translators.
- **Exclusion:** no “West lost, Islam saved” relay myth.

### he-05 — The Mongol Relay

- **Doorway question:** Why does one imperial network leave divergent
  inheritances?
- **Mechanism:** relay, administrative borrowing, destruction, mobility, and
  regional successor institutions.
- **Cases:** China, Persia, and Rus' as a comparison, not a single trajectory.
- **Carrier tension:** shared imperial connection does not imply shared effects
  or equivalent evidence.
- **Reader outcome:** compare divergent transmissions without forcing symmetry.
- **Feasibility:** **hold**; the three-region design exceeds one lecture until a
  multilingual source matrix identifies comparable objects and rival readings.
- **Exclusion:** no unitary “Mongol impact” or civilizational scorecard.

### he-06 — After Zoroaster

- **Doorway question:** What survives a community's political and demographic
  displacement, and through whom?
- **Mechanism:** liturgical transmission, manuscript copying, migration,
  translation, and retrospective reconstruction.
- **Cases:** Zoroastrian textual and communal afterlives in Iran and South Asia.
- **Carrier tension:** late manuscripts can preserve old material without
  supplying an uninterrupted or context-complete record.
- **Reader outcome:** keep textual, ritual, communal, and scholarly survival
  analytically distinct.
- **Feasibility:** **hold** pending specialist and community-grounded source
  review, edition control, and a narrower object than “Zoroastrianism.”
- **Exclusion:** no fossil-religion narrative or unverified quotations.

### he-07 — Two Afterlives of Rome

- **Doorway question:** How can rival projects reactivate the same imperial
  ancestor?
- **Mechanism:** selective genealogy, symbolic appropriation, institutional
  analogy, and political authorization.
- **Cases:** Renaissance humanist Rome and Muscovite “Third Rome.”
- **Carrier tension:** both are reactivations with different objects, audiences,
  and political work—not competing proofs of one continuous Rome.
- **Reader outcome:** compare rival lineages without adjudicating them by
  resemblance alone.
- **Feasibility:** ready for source assembly after fixing one primary text and
  one institutional carrier for each afterlife.
- **Exclusion:** no civilizational ownership contest.

### he-08 — Entropy Is Not Decline

- **Doorway question:** What has the series learned that a universal decay
  narrative cannot explain?
- **Mechanism:** controlled comparison across dimensions of content, context,
  capacity, and provenance.
- **Cases:** only the reviewed cases from he-01 through he-07.
- **Carrier tension:** more administrative or textual order may coexist with
  less recoverable variation, and recovery may coexist with reinvention.
- **Reader outcome:** use entropy as a bounded diagnostic and know when to
  reject it.
- **Feasibility:** **hold** until at least four case lectures pass source and
  value review.
- **Exclusion:** no universal law, ranking, cycle, or predictive equation.

## Drafting Order After the Pilot

Source feasibility suggests `he-02`, then `he-03` or a narrowed `he-04`.
Lectures marked hold must not be forced into prose merely to preserve sequence.
The series order is pedagogical; drafting order follows evidentiary readiness.
